Domestic Violence

Within the four walls of homes, the violence against women is quite high. Domestic violence against women is widely prevalent, but has remained largely invisible. Statistics reveal that 45% of Indian women are slapped, kicked or beaten by their husbands. (ICRW 2002) .32% had committed acts of violence against their pregnant wives. Every 60 minutes a woman dies in India due to domestic violence. Women accept violence because social norms sanction them. At the same time, cultural conditioning and economic dependence prevent the vast majority of women leaving their marital homes. Though there is criminal law to prevent domestic violence, the recent civil law “Protection of women from Domestic Violence Act (October 2006)” is aimed at providing relief, compensation and support to a woman.

    • Forms of Domestic Violence


    Verbal and emotional violence

    • Accusing/insulting you and your parents
    • Insult for not having a male child
    • Preventing your or your child from attending school, college or an educational institution.
    • Preventing you from taking up a job.
    • Forcing you to leave your job.
    • Preventing you from meeting other people.
    • Threat to commit suicide.

    Physical violence

    • Slapping
    • Kicking
    • Punching
    • Causing bodily pain or injury in other manner.

    Economic violence

    • Not providing you money for maintaining yourself and your children.
    • Not providing food,clothes,medicines,etc. for your children
    • Taking away your income from your salary forcibly.
    • Forcing you out of the house you live in.
    • Not paying the rent if you stay in a rented accomodation.

    Sexual violence

    • Forced sexual intercourse
    • Forced you to look at pornography or any other obscene pictures or material
    • Any act of sexual nature to abuse/degrade you which is violating your dignity

    How to deal with Domestic Violence

    Tip: If you are facing domestic abuse, DON'T seek to take "revenge" on those who are trying to harm you.
    It is a futile and endless journey and will leave you and your loved ones much worse off than
    if you just pick up your things, leave and focus on building a new life for you and your children.

    The best revenge is to build a successful life without your abuser.
    • Remember the Government has enacted Domestic Violence Act, which is civil law meant primarily for protection
      orders and not meant to penalize criminally.

    Salient features of Domestic Violence Act

    • Includes physical and mental ill-treatment.
    • Measures against harassment in the form of dowry demands also included in this law.
    • Gives women right to secure housing.
    • Court can also issue protection orders that prevent the abuser to harass the woman.
